# Medicon - Medical Bills Analyzer with AI Precision
Project Overview & Research Paper Reference Guide

## 1. Abstract / Project Overview
Medicon is an advanced, AI-driven healthcare auditing platform designed to protect patients and health insurance providers from medical billing fraud and overcharging. By leveraging cutting-edge Optical Character Recognition (OCR), semantic Natural Language Processing (NLP), and Large Language Models (LLMs), Medicon automatically parses complex hospital invoices and matches them against standardized government rates (such as the Central Government Health Scheme - CGHS). The platform identifies price exaggerations, document inconsistencies, and provides a clear "Fraud Risk Assessment," returning transparency to healthcare billing.

## 2. Core Features (System Capabilities)

### 2.1. Intelligent OCR & Data Extraction
- Multi-format ingestion: Uploads medical bills as Images or PDFs.
- Utilizes Google Cloud Vision API and Google GenAI (Gemini) to robustly extract tabular data, line-item charges, hospital details, and patient information from unstructured or poorly formatted invoices.

### 2.2. Semantic AI Matching & NLP
- Maps non-standard hospital terminology to standardized government (CGHS) procedure codes.
- Uses AI embeddings (`Sentence-Transformers`) and fuzzy string matching (`FuzzyWuzzy`) to establish semantic similarity between bill items and official directories.
- Incorporates a **Manual Override System** (`manual_overrides.json`) allowing domain experts to correct AI mapping errors and continuously improve matching accuracy.

### 2.3. Automated Rate Validation & Overcharge Detection
- Compares individual line items against standard market/CGHS rates based on region and hospital accreditation.
- Automatically calculates the absolute overcharged amount and percentage deviation for each procedure/item.
- Categorizes violations by severity (e.g., High, Medium, Low) to help users prioritize major discrepancies.

### 2.4. Fraud Risk Assessment & Document Consistency
- Generates a holistic "Risk Score" and provides detailed textual justifications for the assigned risk level.
- Automatically performs mathematical checks to ensure document consistency (e.g., verifying if the sum of individual line items matches the stated grand total).
- Flags potential issues like unbundling of services, duplicate billing, and balance billing anomalies.

### 2.5. Hospital Discovery and Verification
- Verifies if the billing hospital is a recognized CGHS-empanelled or NABH-accredited facility.
- Includes API services to search the hospital database by location or specific names.

### 2.6. Interactive & Dynamic Dashboard (UI/UX)
- Features a highly responsive, modern web interface.
- Provides visual data breakdowns, interactive charts (via Recharts), and animated transitions (via Framer Motion) for an intuitive auditing experience.

## 3. Technology Stack

### 3.1. Frontend (User Interface)
- **Framework:** Next.js (Version 16.x) with React (Version 19.x)
- **Styling:** Tailwind CSS (Version 4.x) for utility-first styling.
- **Animations:** Framer Motion for dynamic UI transitions.
- **Icons & Components:** Lucide React, Sonner (Toasts), React-Dropzone (File uploads).
- **Data Visualization:** Recharts
- **Language:** TypeScript

### 3.2. Backend (API & Business Logic)
- **Framework:** FastAPI (Python) running asynchronously via Uvicorn.
- **Data Validation:** Pydantic models for strict type-checking and API schemas.
- **Architecture:** Modular service architecture (`analyzer.py`, `ocr.py`, `validator.py`, `hospital.py`).

### 3.3. Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning
- **Computer Vision / OCR:** Google Cloud Vision API (for text extraction), `pdf2image`, `Pillow`.
- **Large Language Models (LLM):** Google GenAI (`gemini-pro-vision` or equivalent) for intelligent parsing.
- **Natural Language Processing (NLP):** `Sentence-Transformers` (HuggingFace) for dense vector embeddings, Scikit-learn (cosine similarity).
- **String Matching:** `FuzzyWuzzy`, `python-Levenshtein`.

### 3.4. Data Processing & Utilities
- **Data Manipulation:** Pandas, NumPy
- **Geocoding:** Geopy (for hospital locality mapping)
- **Environment Management:** `python-dotenv` for managing sensitive API keys.

## 5. Conclusion (Draft Version)
This paper proposes an intelligent, multi-layered medical billing audit and fraud detection framework. Unlike traditional rule-based systems that struggle with the unstructured and varying terminology of healthcare invoices, the proposed system successfully bridges the gap between loosely-worded hospital bills and standardized government (CGHS) benchmark codes. The proposed architecture leverages a robust pipeline combining Google Cloud Vision for Optical Character Recognition (OCR), Google GenAI for intelligent tabular parsing, and a hybrid semantic matching engine.

A core methodological contribution of this research is the system's hybrid matching architecture, which utilizes:
1. **Large Language Model (LLM)-based entity extraction** (Gemini) to structure raw invoice data.
2. **Dense vector embeddings** (`Sentence-Transformers`) and fuzzy string matching (`FuzzyWuzzy`) for establishing semantic similarity between varying hospital nomenclature and standard code directories.
3. **Automated rate validation** against Central Government Health Scheme (CGHS) directories dynamically scaled by hospital accreditations (e.g., NABH vs. Non-NABH).
4. **Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) Manual Overrides** (`manual_overrides.json`) allowing domain experts to continuously refine mapping accuracy and mitigate semantic drift.

This multi-faceted approach effectively categorizes overcharges by severity and scales efficiently via a modern technology stack (FastAPI backend and Next.js frontend). The system's risk assessment goes beyond individual procedure overcharging by incorporating structural document checks—validating grand totals against the sum of individual line items to detect mathematical inconsistencies, hidden fees, or duplicate charges. By transitioning fraud detection from slow, post-claim administrative processing to near real-time automated auditing, the proposed framework provides an actionable, transparent, and robust solution for mitigating pricing opacity and protecting patients from arbitrary healthcare overcharging.

## 6. Future Scope (Draft Version)
While the current prototype successfully categorizes risk and detects overcharges autonomously, several technical enhancements can further its practical readiness and generalization.

### 6.1 Multilingual OCR and Regional Adaptation
Currently, the extraction pipeline is highly optimized for printed English medical bills. Future work will explore adapting the OCR and generative extraction layers for multilingual and code-mixed Indian languages to process regional hospital bills effectively. Integrating advanced document layout models (such as LayoutLM) could further improve resilience against low-quality scans and handwritten annotations.

### 6.2 Dynamic Knowledge Graphs for Unbundling Detection
The current semantic matching system can be expanded into a comprehensive healthcare knowledge graph connecting procedures, diagnosis codes, medications, and regional hospital accreditations. Implementing Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) could facilitate complex relational reasoning, helping identify mutually exclusive procedures and sophisticated unbundling patterns beyond item-level semantic similarity.

### 6.3 Advanced Explainability (XAI) and Legal Interpretability
Although the system already provides categorized risk scoring and text-based justifications for document inconsistencies, incorporating advanced Explainable AI (XAI) techniques—like feature attribution scoring and counterfactual explanations—could enhance the evidentiary use of the system. Structuring these AI explanations into formal legal reasoning templates may aid consumer dispute resolution tribunals and insurance claim settlements.

### 6.4 Institutional and EHR Integration
Further research should investigate integrating the auditing module directly into hospital Electronic Health Record (EHR) pipelines and insurance claim gateways. Such integration would shift the paradigm from reactive fraud resolution to proactive billing compliance pre-discharge.

### 6.5 Privacy-Preserving Architectures
Given the sensitive nature of Personal Health Information (PHI) in billing data, future deployments could implement federated learning architectures and differential privacy constraints. This would allow the overarching semantic models to learn from decentralized hospital data without centralizing raw, unencrypted patient identifiers, aligning with stringent data protection regulations such as the DPDP Act.
